Five quick questions. Short answers are fine — we're looking for your perspective, not an essay.

**2. What are your hero dishes / the things you most want to feature?**

Pick 1–2: **Awareness** (reach new people who don't know you yet) / **Sales** (drive reservations) / **Community** (deepen loyalty with existing guests) / **Growth** (more followers, more reach) / **Launches** (promote new dishes or events)

## Part 2 — Assets to Send

These are what make the difference between content that looks like Sidewalk Bistro and content that looks like any restaurant.

**Must-haves:**
- [ ] **Brand colours** — hex codes if you have them; descriptions work too ("deep navy, warm cream, burgundy")
- [ ] **Font names** — the fonts used in your logo and menus, if you know them
- [ ] **Logo file** — PNG with transparent background preferred
- [ ] **Food photos** — original high-res photos of your hero dishes (crêpes, steak frites, etc.). These are the anchor of every visual — the dish must look exactly right, not AI-approximated. Clean shots on neutral or dark background work best, but any good-quality originals are fine.

**Really helpful to have:**
- [ ] **Lifestyle shots** — the dining room, table settings, candles, the street view, BYOW moments
- [ ] **Brand guidelines or Canva Brand Kit** — if you have one, it'll fill in most of the visual questions below automatically
- [ ] **5–10 example posts** — screenshots of posts from your feed you're happiest with (used for style reference, not production)
- [ ] **Accounts you admire** — 2–3 restaurant or food accounts whose visual style you like, even if they're very different from you
- [ ] **Accounts you'd never want to look like** — any style that feels wrong for Sidewalk Bistro
