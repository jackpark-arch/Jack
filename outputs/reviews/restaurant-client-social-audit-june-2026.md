## Audit Framework

The following will be analyzed once data is received. This section documents what the audit covers so the client understands what they are enabling.

### Account health
- Follower count and 90-day growth rate
- Reach and impressions trends
- Profile visit rate (visits ÷ reach — indicates brand discovery interest)
- Overall engagement rate vs. restaurant industry benchmarks (see below)

### Content performance
- Top 5 posts by saves (Instagram) or engagement rate (other platforms) — with analysis of why they worked
- Bottom 3 posts — with honest diagnosis of what went wrong
- Performance by content type: single image, carousel, reel, video
- Performance by content theme: food photography, behind-the-scenes, promotions, community, staff

### Posting cadence
- How many posts per week on average
- Consistency — any gaps or irregular weeks
- Best days and times based on own engagement data
- Platform mix — which platforms are active vs neglected

### Audience
- Follower demographics if available (age, gender, location)
- Audience growth sources (hashtags, explore, home feed, direct search)
- Any audience quality signals (comments with questions vs. generic "nice" comments)

## Restaurant Industry Benchmarks

Use these as baselines when reviewing performance data. Benchmarks are averages and vary by account size — a smaller account (under 5K followers) will typically see higher engagement rates than a large account.

| Platform | Metric | Restaurant Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Engagement rate (per post) | 1.5–3.5% |
| Instagram | Saves per post (under 5K followers) | 5–20 |
| Instagram | Reel average reach | 2–5× account size |
| Facebook | Engagement rate | 0.5–1.5% |
| TikTok | Video completion rate | 30–50% |
| TikTok | Engagement rate | 3–8% |
| Google Business | Review response rate | >90% target |
| All platforms | Optimal posting frequency (restaurant) | Instagram: 4–7×/week; Facebook: 3–5×/week; TikTok: 3–5×/week |

**Key note:** For restaurants, **saves are the most important Instagram metric.** Saves indicate the audience wants to return to the content (e.g., a recipe, a menu item they want to try, a reservation they are planning). The Instagram algorithm also weights saves heavily in its ranking signals.

## Preliminary Observations

Based on the task description only — full analysis requires data.

### What is known
- The client is a restaurant being onboarded for social media strategy
- No prior social media strategy or content calendar is in place (context files do not yet exist)
- The brand onboarding task is in progress (goals and objectives not yet finalised)

### Common starting-point challenges for restaurant social media
1. **Inconsistent posting cadence** — most restaurants post in bursts around events and slow down mid-week. Consistency matters more than volume.
2. **Over-promotion** — restaurant accounts that lead with promotional content (sales, discounts) see lower engagement than those that lead with food content, behind-the-scenes, or stories.
3. **Underuse of reels** — Instagram's algorithm actively deprioritises static images for discovery. Restaurants that are not using reels are not reaching new audiences.
4. **No clear content pillars** — without defined pillars (e.g., seasonal menu spotlights, chef stories, sourcing, customer moments), content defaults to random daily specials that don't compound over time.
5. **Google Business Profile neglect** — for restaurants, Google Business is often the highest-intent discovery channel (people searching "restaurants near me"). Review management and photo updates are often neglected.
