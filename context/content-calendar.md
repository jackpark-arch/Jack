**Platform:** Instagram (primary) + Facebook Event creation  
**Pillar:** BYOW Wednesday Series  
**Format:** Single image  
**Objective:** Engagement — relaunch the BYOW series as a summer institution

**Topic:** BYOW Wednesday — the summer series officially begins

**Angle:** The official re-introduction of BYOW Wednesday as a recurring summer tradition. "Every Wednesday, the wine is yours. The food is ours." Warm and inviting — not salesy. This is a ritual, not a promotion.

**Visual direction:** A wine bottle (unlabelled or guest-generic) resting on a Sidewalk Bistro table — candlelight, white tablecloth, bistro chairs soft-focused in background. Warm amber tones. Intimate. No people needed.

**Caption direction:** Introduce the series in first person ("Every Wednesday this summer..."). Include a guest CTA: "Tag us with your Wednesday bottle — we'll feature you." Link to reservation in bio.

**Notes:** This is the Series launch post — set the template for all future BYOW Wednesdays. Also create a recurring Facebook Event: "BYOW Wednesdays — Summer 2026" with weekly recurrence. Reply to all comments on this post within 24 hours.

### POST 2
**Week:** 1 | **Date:** Thu Jul 3 | **Day:** Thursday  
**Platform:** Instagram (primary) + TikTok (cross-post)  
**Pillar:** Behind the Scenes / Kitchen  
**Format:** Reel (30–45 seconds)  
**Objective:** Awareness — reach new audiences via the Reels algorithm

**Topic:** "How we make the crêpe suzette — a 30-second French kitchen moment"

**Angle:** First-person kitchen perspective. The tableside theatre of crêpe suzette — the flambé as the hero moment. No narration needed. Let the ambient kitchen audio, the sizzle, the flame carry it. End on the finished dish.

**Visual direction:** Close-up of hands folding crêpes, orange zest being grated, warm copper pan, flambé flame caught in the final 5 seconds. End frame: plated crêpe suzette with the bistro dining room blurred behind it.

**Caption direction:** "Some things are just better done in person. Crêpe suzette — every night at Sidewalk Bistro. Reserve via link in bio."

**Notes:** This is the FIRST REEL ever posted by this account — prioritize quality. Film during a quiet prep service, not peak rush. Use ambient kitchen audio or an understated French instrumental. Cross-post to TikTok on the same day as the account launch post. Save the raw footage — it may be usable in future compilations.

### POST 3

**Platform:** Instagram + Facebook  
**Pillar:** Food Photography  
**Format:** Single image  
**Objective:** Engagement / Enquiries — drive weekend bookings

**Topic:** "Steak frites — the dish that started it all"

**Angle:** Origin story angle. Position steak frites as the dish that defines Sidewalk Bistro — before the waterfront reputation, before BYOW, this is what brought people back. Nostalgic and grounding.

**Visual direction:** Hero overhead shot of steak frites — the steak slightly rested, frites golden and piled, French mustard on the side, a glass of red wine catching the light at the edge of the frame. Warm, moody lighting. Not studio-clean — it should feel like it was shot at the table.

**Caption direction:** A short, confident 2-sentence opener about the dish. "Still our most-ordered. Still the reason people come back." Booking nudge in the third line.

**Notes:** Saturday = highest organic reach window on Instagram. Also share to Facebook page. No Independence Day tie-in needed unless the client wants a playful French-American angle ("Even in America, steak frites wins").

### POST 6
**Week:** 2 | **Date:** Fri Jul 11 | **Day:** Friday  
**Platform:** Instagram + TikTok  
**Pillar:** Food Photography  
**Format:** Carousel (6 slides) on Instagram; adapted Reel on TikTok  
**Objective:** Engagement — target saves (educational content)

**Topic:** "The French aperitif hour — how to drink like a Parisian before dinner"

**Angle:** Educational + aspirational. Turn a French cultural tradition into shareable content. Slide breakdown: (1) hook — "The French don't have pre-dinner drinks. They have an aperitif." (2) What aperitif actually is (3) Classic choices: Kir, Pastis, Suze (4) The ritual — the ritual matters as much as the drink (5) How to recreate it at home (6) "Or let us do it for you — reserve your table."

**Visual direction:** Elegant flat-lay: two aperitif glasses, olives in a small dish, a few French crackers, an unlabelled bottle. Soft, diffused golden-hour light. Clean, editorial. Feels like a French lifestyle magazine.

**Caption direction:** Educational tone throughout. Keep each slide to 1–2 lines. Final CTA slide: warm invitation, not hard sell.

**Notes:** High save potential — this is the kind of content people save to "try later." TikTok version: adapt as a 45-second voiceover or talking-head with the same content beats. Film TikTok version with the chef or owner if possible — human presence works better on TikTok than carousels.
